Predictions vs Trades: The AI evaluates ~30 candidates every cycle but only trades 0-3. A "prediction" is the AI's directional call on ANY candidate (traded or not). A "resolved prediction" means we checked later whether the price moved in the predicted direction — the AI was right (win) or wrong (loss). This measures forecasting accuracy across everything the AI evaluates, not just what it trades. The self-tuner needs 20+ resolved predictions before it starts adjusting parameters. A prediction resolves when the price moves 2%+ in either direction, or after 3-10 trading days.

Risk Budget — per-position variance contribution

Each position contributes weight × annualized_vol to the book's variance. Equal-dollar weights are not equal-risk weights — a 5% slug of a 60%-vol biotech contributes ~3× the variance of a 5% slug of a 20%-vol utility. Names ≥ 2× or ≤ 0.5× the per-position average are flagged. Sizing rule the AI is told: size ∝ 25% / annualized_vol, clamped [0.4×, 1.6×].
