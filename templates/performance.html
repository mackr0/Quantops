Capacity Analysis — Where Each Strategy Saturates

{% if scaling_capacity %}

At what capital does each strategy stop fitting in its universe? "Capacity Wall" is the capital level at which an avg-sized position would be ~5% of typical daily volume in that profile's universe — the rule-of-thumb threshold above which you start moving the price when you trade. Slippage at the wall comes from sqrt(capital growth) × current measured slippage. This is about whether execution still works at scale, NOT whether the edge survives — that's unknowable from a single capital data point.

How this is calculated: For each profile we read its real measured avg position size, then assume that position size scales linearly with capital (constant pos_pct = avg_position ÷ current_capital). The capacity wall hits when avg_position would reach 5% of typical daily volume in the profile's universe — beyond that, your own order size starts pushing the price. Tier ADV constants used: Small Cap ~$20M, Mid Cap ~$200M, Large Cap ~$2B. These are stylized averages, not the exact ADV of the names this profile trades, so treat the wall as a rough breakpoint (within 2-3×) not a hard number. Slippage at the wall is sqrt(growth) × your current measured slippage — just the existing market-impact math at the higher capital level.

What this does NOT tell you: Whether the strategy's edge still exists at higher capital. We only have one data point (your current size). If small-cap edges depend on names big money can't touch, the edge could erode well before the capacity wall says you can. {% else %}
